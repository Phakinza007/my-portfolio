# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective clients and hiring managers evaluating Phakin Chawanpunya as a frontend developer for hire. They land here from a resume link, GitHub, or a direct portfolio share, scanning quickly to judge craft and range before deciding to reach out. For the three clinic/wellness demo pages specifically, the audience widens to include small-business owners (dental clinics, spas, aesthetic clinics) imagining Phakin building something similar for them — the pages double as proof-of-capability samples aimed at that vertical.

## Product Purpose

A single-page developer portfolio (vanilla HTML/CSS/JS, GitHub Pages) that showcases 21+ project cards, case studies, and a resume, with the explicit goal of generating freelance/full-time hiring interest. Success = a visitor clicks through to a project, reads a case study, or submits the contact form. The three newest pages (BRIGHT Dental, SERENE Wellness Retreat, VELVÉ Aesthetics) exist specifically to attract clinic/wellness-industry clients by demonstrating that kind of site convincingly.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, modern, precise. The site-wide shell is a dark, GitHub-inspired developer aesthetic (Outfit type, near-black surfaces, blue accent) that signals technical credibility. Individual project pages carry their own committed visual identity (e.g. teal-medical for the dental clinic, earthy-luxury for the spa, violet-modern for the aesthetic clinic) rather than reusing the shell's dark palette — each is a self-contained demo proving range.

## Anti-references

- Generic stock-photo clinic sites: smiling-dentist-with-thumbs-up, stethoscope-close-up, hands-in-blue-gloves cliché medical stock photography.
- Template SaaS card grids: identical icon + heading + text cards with no hierarchy.
- Any card thumbnail that looks like a screenshot crop rather than a designed preview.

## Design Principles

- Each project page commits to its own palette and typography; do not default every page back to the dark developer shell.
- Real, specific imagery over generic stock photography — if a clinic/spa page needs a photo, it should read as a specific person/place, not a mood-board placeholder.
- Card thumbnails on the index must telegraph the target page's identity at a glance (CSS mini-UI echoes or curated real imagery), not a random screenshot.
- Every new page must remain overflow-free at 375px and keep Lighthouse at 100/100/100.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` on all scroll/reveal animations (already implemented site-wide). No accessibility requirements beyond AA have been specified by the owner.
