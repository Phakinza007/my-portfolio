LaunchLedger makes launch money visible.

A startup budget dashboard concept for teams that need to see runway, spend pressure, and launch decisions before the next campaign sprint.

View live project Project source Back to case studies
Project type Finance dashboard concept
Audience Small launch and startup teams
Focus Runway, budget, and decisions
Build Static HTML and CSS
problem

Launch planning feels risky when budget signals are split across tools.

A small team might track runway in a spreadsheet, campaign costs in another file, and decisions in chat. That makes it hard to know what needs attention before money is spent.

goal

Give the team one page for budget confidence.

The concept needed a fast overview of remaining runway, paid-test pressure, spend categories, and a decision queue so the user can act without reading a long financial report.

design decisions

Three choices that made finance easier to scan.

The page avoids a heavy finance look and focuses on practical startup launch decisions.

  1. 01

    Runway is the lead signal.

    The strongest visual weight goes to the time left, because runway explains why every budget and campaign decision matters.

  2. 02

    Spend categories are grouped by action.

    Paid tests, production, software, and team costs are separated so a reviewer can see where decisions would happen in the interface.

  3. 03

    Decision queue turns numbers into next steps.

    The dashboard includes a short queue for actions like delay ads or protect runway, so the page feels operational instead of purely decorative.
